Political Token Encoding in AI and Behavior Analysis

Delete this step?

Have people tried developed political token encoding in a space similar to encodings used in generative AI chatbots? Have the done this for personality, consumer behavior, demographics, political ideology, political messaging, political beliefs and actual voting behavior? I'm interested in both classical statistical techniques and modern AI methods, as well as their comparative performance and data requirements. Prefer to base your answer on empirical academic studies or reports from credible institutes like Pew and others. However, do not exclude other sources if they offer a unique perspective.

Recent research explores the intersection of AI and political profiling. Studies demonstrate that personalized political ads tailored to individuals' personalities are more effective than non-personalized ones, raising ethical concerns about AI-driven microtargeting (Simchon et al., 2024). Advanced AI techniques, including large language models, have been used to create political avatars capable of emulating real politicians' responses in voting advice applications, offering a more nuanced approach to political analysis (Mancera Andrade & Terán, 2024). The effectiveness of political chatbots in influencing voting intentions has been examined, revealing that anthropomorphism and message type interact with political ideology to affect outcomes (Kim & Lee, 2023). Additionally, novel Bayesian-network models combining demographic, behavioral, and social features have shown promise in predicting voting behavior in social networks, even for users who don't disclose their political preferences online (Idan & Feigenbaum, 2019).

The persuasive effects of political microtargeting in the age of generative artificial intelligence
Almog Simchon +2
PNAS Nexus
2024 ·
13 citations
PDFDOI
Personalized political ads tailored to individuals' personalities are more effective than non-personalized ads, highlighting the potential risks of using AI and microtargeting for political messaging.
1. A corpus of 1,552 political ads published on Facebook to UK users between December 2019 and December 2021. The researchers selected 10 ads from this corpus for their studies. 2. The researchers used a language model to assign "openness scores" to the 1,552 ads, which they then used to select 10 ad pairs for studies 2a and 2b. 3. For studies 2a and 2b, the researchers generated additional ads using language models (GPT-3 and ChatGPT) and selected 10 ad pairs from each model that maximized the difference in openness scores. They then ran experiments with 803 and 804 participants, respectively, to test the effectiveness of these generated ads.
