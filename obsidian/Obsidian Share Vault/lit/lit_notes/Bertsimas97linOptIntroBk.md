>> **Abstract**
> This graduate-level textbook provides a rigorous, self-contained introduction to linear optimization (LO). It covers the expressive abilities of LO, the geometry and structure of polyhedral sets, LO duality and its applications, classical algorithms including primal and dual simplex and network simplex methods, polynomial-time solvability via the ellipsoid algorithm, and conic programming with an emphasis on second order and semidefinite optimization. The book is intended for senior undergraduate and graduate students building and processing optimization models, and serves as the main textbook for a semester-long graduate course on linear optimization[6][7].
